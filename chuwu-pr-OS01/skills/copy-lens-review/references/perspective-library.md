# Perspective Library

Use these default perspectives unless the user gives a more specific audience list.

| Perspective | Focus | Common Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ordinary public users | Common sense and public-language norms | Low taste, unclear intention, overdone joke |
| Target consumers | Brand trust and category fit | Brand feels unstable or opportunistic |
| Women users | Respect, objectification, male gaze, stereotypes | Women reduced to relation, body, appearance, or pleasing others |
| Mothers | Respect for motherhood and personal identity | Mother image consumed as a topic prop |
| Married women | Marriage boundaries and intimate terms | Marriage relation trivialized |
| Male spouses / fathers | Family role and relation framing | Family members used as awkward punchline |
| Young users | Internet fluency and taste | Brand tries too hard to be young |
| Middle-aged and older users | Literal reading and tradition | Slang becomes incomprehensible or offensive |
| Parents of minors | Value demonstration and language boundary | Bad example for children |
| Circle insiders | Whether subculture terms are accurate | Culture is misused or stigmatized |
| Circle outsiders | Whether copy works without insider knowledge | Copy is read literally |
| Gender-issue sensitive users | Structural bias and identity framing | Critique around objectification or stereotypes |
| Conservative-value users | Family ethics and social order | Challenge to marriage/family norms |
| Media / opinion leaders | Public issue potential | Turns into brand value and public responsibility article |
| Brand loyalists | Long-term trust | Brand damages affinity for short-term attention |

## Pet Food Extensions

Use when reviewing cat/dog food, treats, supplements, or pet-brand copy. See `pet-food-risk-context.md` for minefields.

| Perspective | Focus | Common Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dedicated pet owners (铲屎官) | Pet health, ingredient transparency, emotional bond | Guilt-tripping, fear-mongering, ingredient bait-and-switch |
| Ingredient-focused owners | Label accuracy, meat source, additives | "Fresh meat" claims vs meat meal reality |
| Veterinarians / clinical viewers | Medical boundaries, prescription diet rules | Disease claims, DIY treatment encouragement |
| Animal welfare observers | Pet portrayal, breed bias, rescue ethics | Staged suffering, breed shaming, exploitative stories |
| Pet-food skeptics ("毒粮" watchers) | Fraud, false claims, industry distrust | Any absolute promise becomes screenshot fuel |
| Breeder / experienced keepers | Life-stage and species fit | Cat/dog mismatch, wrong life-stage messaging |

## Red Team Critics

Use at least five:

1. Social media critic: crops the most provocative line and removes brand explanation.
2. Gender-issue blogger: reads the copy through objectification, stereotypes, and power relations.
3. Conservative parent: reads through children, family ethics, and festival respect.
4. Media commentator: turns the case into public expression and brand responsibility.
5. Competitor PR: contrasts the brand against a safer alternative.

For each critic, output:

- attack angle;
- likely public post;
- spread potential: low / medium / high / crisis.
